      What You're Looking At

      This is a working prototype of Change Log // Nashville‚Äîa civic platform designed to make decisions visible, patterns trackable, and public participation possible. It's a living, open-source tool built for the people who are most affected by public policy but often last to be informed or consulted.

      Here's what you'll find:

      • Legislation tracking, with accessible summaries of what's being proposed, passed, or postponed
      • Metro spending and contracts, showing where local funds go and who benefits
      • Contextual issue threads, to help tie together decisions across time, agencies, and neighborhoods
      • Discussion tools, so informed public input doesn't just live in group chats or vanish after 20 minutes of council comment

      This isn't a dashboard for policy wonks. It's a prototype for civic infrastructure we can actually use.

      Why This Exists

      The decisions shaping Nashville are technically public‚Äîbut scattered, fragmented, and often impossible to follow unless you already know where to look. Meeting minutes are delayed, spending reports are buried in PDFs, and legislative text is rarely written for non-lawyers.

      Neighborhood conversations are happening‚Äîbut they're happening in private Signal chats, Facebook threads, and hallway sidebars, not in any shared public record. That means we lose civic memory. We repeat fights. We miss the patterns.

      Change Log // Nashville exists to:

      • Track decisions while they're unfolding
      • Make power legible‚Äîwhere it flows, how it's used, and who it impacts
      • Connect local knowledge and public data in one place

      We need infrastructure that can hold complexity and invite feedback. This is our first attempt.

      What's Happening Behind the Scenes

      This prototype pulls real data from Metro Nashville sources‚Äîlegislation, budgets, contracts‚Äîand organizes it in a way designed for use, not obfuscation. The design is grounded in systems thinking, not party politics.

      We're building around a set of themes that we believe help societies thrive:

      • Governing Without Coercion
      • Feedback & Truth-Telling
      • Decision-Making Beyond Binaries
      • Contextual Leadership
      • Ritual, Myth & Meaning
      • Circulation of Power & Resources
      • Structural & Planetary Care

      These themes shape how we tag, explain, and connect the pieces.

      What's Next?

      This is an early build. With support, we can:

      • Expand coverage beyond Metro Council to include planning, schools, and more
      • Add search and sort tools to track issues over time
      • Build participatory feedback tools for neighborhood groups, organizers, and researchers

      This is powered by volunteers and small donations right now. If you want to help, you can:

      • Support with a one-time or recurring donation
      • Offer research, tech, or editorial time
      • Share feedback or help us test the tool

      We're building this because we needed it and no one else was. If you feel the same, we'd love to build it with you.
